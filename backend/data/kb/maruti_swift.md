# Maruti Suzuki Swift Service Guide

## Oil and Filter Change

The Swift's small-displacement petrol engines commonly run on 5W-30
semi-synthetic or full-synthetic oil. A 10,000 km or 12-month interval is
typical, though many owners in dense urban traffic follow a more
conservative 5,000 km interval given the stop-and-go conditions common in
the markets where the Swift is popular.

## Brake Service

Front brake pads typically last 20,000-30,000 km in heavy city driving,
reflecting frequent braking cycles. Brake fluid is commonly replaced every
2 years to maintain consistent pedal feel and prevent internal corrosion of
the brake master cylinder.

## Coolant Service

Long-life coolant on the Swift is commonly rated for around 5 years/100,000
km before its first change, a fairly standard interval for this vehicle
segment.

## Common Issues

A commonly reported issue is premature clutch wear on manual-transmission
Swifts driven mostly in heavy traffic, since frequent partial clutch
engagement accelerates wear. Some owners report a rattling exhaust heat
shield at idle on higher-mileage units, usually resolved by tightening or
replacing the shield's mounting clips.
