# Honda CR-V Service Guide

## Oil and Filter Change

The CR-V's turbocharged four-cylinder engine typically specifies 0W-20
full-synthetic oil. A 10,000 km or 12-month interval is standard for normal
use; Honda's maintenance-minder system commonly flags an earlier change on
vehicles used for towing, off-road driving, or frequent short trips.

## Brake Service

Front pads on a CR-V generally last 35,000-45,000 km, somewhat less than a
sedan of similar size due to the extra weight of the SUV body and any towed
loads. Brake fluid is commonly flushed every 3 years to prevent moisture
absorption from degrading pedal feel and corroding brake lines.

## Coolant Service

Long-life coolant in the CR-V is typically rated for about 10 years/192,000
km before the first change, after which a 5-year interval is a common
conservative recommendation.

## Common Issues

A commonly reported issue on CR-V models with the turbocharged engine is
oil dilution with fuel, especially in colder climates with mostly short
trips - owners are commonly advised to take longer highway drives
periodically to let the engine reach full operating temperature and burn
off diluted fuel. A musty air-conditioning odor on start-up has also been
widely reported, usually traced to evaporator core condensation combined
with cabin air filter buildup.
