# Hyundai Creta Service Guide

## Oil and Filter Change

The Creta's petrol and diesel engines are commonly specified for 5W-30
full-synthetic oil. A 10,000 km or 12-month interval is typical for petrol
variants under normal use, while diesel variants are commonly serviced
every 10,000 km or 12 months as well, though heavy-duty or dusty-condition
use is usually shortened to roughly every 7,500 km.

## Brake Service

Front brake pads typically last 25,000-35,000 km in dense city driving
common in the markets where the Creta sells heavily, and can stretch to
45,000 km with more highway use. Brake fluid replacement every 2 years is a
common conservative recommendation.

## Coolant Service

Long-life coolant is commonly rated for around 5 years/100,000 km on the
Creta before its first change, reflecting the shorter intervals typical of
this vehicle segment compared to premium sedans.

## Common Issues

A commonly reported issue is a whining noise from the timing chain area on
early diesel variants at cold start, generally linked to chain tensioner
wear and addressed under warranty on affected model years. Infotainment
touchscreen unresponsiveness after long idle periods has also been widely
reported, usually resolved with a firmware update.
