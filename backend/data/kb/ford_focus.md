# Ford Focus Service Guide

## Oil and Filter Change

The Focus's EcoBoost petrol engines typically call for 5W-20 or 5W-30
full-synthetic oil, with a normal-use interval of around 12,000-16,000 km
or 12 months. Vehicles used mostly for short journeys or frequent idling
are commonly serviced on a shorter, "severe" interval closer to 8,000 km.

## Brake Service

Front brake pads typically last 30,000-40,000 km in mixed driving. Brake
fluid is commonly recommended for replacement every 2 years, since Ford's
DOT 4 spec fluid gradually absorbs moisture that lowers its boiling point.

## Coolant Service

The Focus commonly ships with an orange, long-life coolant rated for
roughly 10 years/240,000 km on its first service interval, dropping to a
5-year interval afterward as the corrosion inhibitors deplete.

## Common Issues

Older dual-clutch "PowerShift" automatic transmissions on the Focus have a
well-documented history of clutch shudder and hesitation complaints,
usually addressed via a transmission control module software update or
clutch pack replacement under extended warranty programs. A common minor
complaint is a rattling heat shield near the exhaust manifold on
higher-mileage units.
