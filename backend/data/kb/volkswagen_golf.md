# Volkswagen Golf Service Guide

## Oil and Filter Change

The Golf's TSI turbo-petrol engines specify VW-approved 0W-20 or 5W-40
long-life full-synthetic oil, with a common service interval of 15,000 km
or 12 months under Volkswagen's "Longlife" regime, dropping to a fixed
10,000 km interval under the "Standard" regime commonly used for
short-trip or severe-condition driving.

## Brake Service

Front brake pads typically last 30,000-40,000 km. Brake fluid is commonly
flushed every 2 years regardless of mileage, per VW's DOT 4 fluid spec,
since moisture absorption is the primary factor in fluid degradation
rather than distance driven.

## Coolant Service

VW's G13 long-life coolant is commonly rated for the vehicle's lifetime
under normal conditions, though many independent shops recommend a
precautionary flush every 5 years/150,000 km to guard against water pump
seal and radiator seam corrosion as the vehicle ages.

## Common Issues

A widely reported issue on Golf models with the DSG dual-clutch automatic
is a mechatronic unit fault triggering harsh or delayed shifts, most common
on higher-mileage units that missed the DSG fluid and filter service
(commonly recommended every 60,000 km). Carbon build-up on intake valves is
also a commonly reported issue on direct-injection TSI engines, since fuel
no longer washes the back of the intake valves as it does in port-injected
engines.
