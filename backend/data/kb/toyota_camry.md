# Toyota Camry Service Guide

## Oil and Filter Change

The Camry's four-cylinder and V6 petrol engines both call for full-synthetic
0W-16 or 0W-20 oil. A 10,000 km or 12-month oil and filter change interval
is standard under normal use; severe conditions (short trips, stop-and-go
traffic, extreme heat) drop that to roughly every 8,000 km.

## Brake Service

Front pads typically last 40,000-50,000 km on a Camry driven mostly on
highways, and closer to 25,000-30,000 km in heavy city traffic. Brake fluid
is commonly flushed every 2 years to keep moisture content low and maintain
consistent pedal feel.

## Coolant Service

The Camry ships with a long-life coolant good for roughly 160,000 km or 10
years on its first service interval. Subsequent changes are commonly
scheduled every 5 years or 80,000 km to keep additive corrosion inhibitors
effective.

## Common Issues

Some Camry model years have reported a "sludge" build-up when oil changes
were consistently skipped or delayed past 15,000 km. A minor but common
complaint is a dashboard rattle at idle traced to a loose trim clip. Hybrid
Camry variants can trigger a hybrid battery cooling-fan warning if the
intake vent behind the rear seat gets blocked by cargo.
