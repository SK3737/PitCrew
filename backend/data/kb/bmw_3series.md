# BMW 3 Series Service Guide

## Oil and Filter Change

BMW's petrol and diesel engines in the 3 Series specify BMW Longlife-01 or
LL-04 full-synthetic oil. Rather than a fixed mileage, BMW's Condition
Based Service system commonly schedules an oil change somewhere between
16,000-24,000 km or 12-24 months based on driving style, with harder
driving or frequent short trips shortening the interval automatically.

## Brake Service

Front brake pads typically last 30,000-40,000 km, with BMW's brake-pad
wear sensor commonly triggering a dashboard warning before a hard mileage
limit is reached. Brake fluid is commonly flushed every 2 years regardless
of mileage, a fixed interval BMW does not vary with the condition-based
system since fluid degradation depends mainly on time and moisture, not
distance.

## Coolant Service

BMW's factory long-life coolant is commonly rated for around 4-6 years or
100,000-150,000 km, after which a full flush is recommended to protect the
aluminium engine block and plastic coolant system components from
corrosion as seals age.

## Common Issues

A widely reported issue on N20/N26-generation turbocharged 3 Series engines
is timing chain and tensioner wear leading to a rattle on cold start, which
in some cases required chain replacement under extended warranty. Plastic
coolant system components (expansion tank, thermostat housing) are also
commonly reported to become brittle and crack with age, making a
higher-than-usual coolant top-up frequency a common early warning sign.
