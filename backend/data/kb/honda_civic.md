# Honda Civic Service Guide

## Oil and Filter Change

The Civic's petrol engines are commonly specified for 0W-20 full-synthetic
oil, with an oil and filter change every 10,000 km or 12 months under
normal use. Honda's maintenance-minder system on many model years shortens
this automatically based on real driving conditions rather than a fixed
mileage alone, so short-trip drivers often see the reminder fire earlier
than 10,000 km.

## Brake Service

Front brake pads on a Civic typically last 35,000-45,000 km in mixed
driving. Brake fluid is commonly replaced every 3 years or 60,000 km as a
conservative interval, since Honda's own fluid spec is DOT 3 which absorbs
moisture faster than DOT 4.

## Coolant Service

Honda's factory long-life coolant is typically rated for around 10
years/192,000 km on the first change for many recent Civic model years.
Owners who mix coolant types (e.g. topping up with a non-Honda-spec
coolant) are commonly advised to shorten this interval to avoid additive
incompatibility.

## Common Issues

A common owner report on turbocharged Civic engines is oil dilution from
fuel in cold climates with frequent short trips, which is why Honda
recommends closer monitoring of the oil life indicator rather than a
blanket fixed interval. Infotainment freezes/reboots have been reported on
some model years, usually resolved by a software update. A/C compressor
clutch chatter at idle is a frequently reported minor issue.
